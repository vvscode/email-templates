Leverage auction results

Collectrium makes it easier than ever to stay up-to-date on your artists’ performance at auction. We track over 500,000 artists at 250 auction houses worldwide. Understand the value of your collection with access to the pulse of the art market.

There are two ways to view recent auction results related to an artist with Collectrium.

1. Select the ‘Auction Results’ tab from within the object view.
2. From the right-hand menu, choose ‘Artists’. Select an artist, click on the ‘Auction Results’ tab, and begin viewing the auction results related to this artist.

If you have an artist in your collection that is not in our database, the auction results tab will be blank. We’re adding new artists daily, so if there's an artist you feel should be included, please contact us directly at support@collectrium.com.

Customize your account to your needs

Collectrium allows you to tailor elements of your system to your specific needs such as your preferred unit of measurement, default currency, and exchange rates. You can also upload your own letterhead for use on reports, invoices, and forms.

1. Click on the person icon in the top left-hand corner of the Collectrium window, and choose ‘Settings’ from the dropdown .
2. In the Settings area, select the ‘Application Settings’ tab.
3. Here's where you can change your preferred currency, unit of measurement, etc. with the click of a button.
4. You can also add and preview your letterhead by clicking on 'Add Image' next to 'Report Logo'

That’s it! Collectrium will automatically convert dimensions and financial values based on these settings, as well as give you the option to include your letterhead on forms and reports.

Track the location of your objects with precision

Do you know where your objects are? If you have multiple residences, utilize storage facilities, and loan or consign objects, Collectrium can help you track the precise location of your assets.

How to set-up a location/sublocation, and then associate it with an object:

1. Select 'Locations' from the left-hand menu, and then click 'Add location.' Name your location and fill in the relevant details.
2. To more accurately track your objects within a location, you can create any number of sublocations: click on a location and then choose 'Add Sublocation.'

Organize your objects

Organize your objects in groups and sections:

Definitions:

• Sections are top-level groupings to visually organize your objects. Sections contains groups. For example, a section could be Exhibitions, Inventory, or a type of object.
• Groups are folders to place your objects in. For example, a group could be the name of an exhibition, the name of an artist, or a period.

How to create sections and groups:

1. On the top right of your account homepage, you can easily create and name new sections and groups by selecting 'New Section' or 'New Group.'
2. When you add a new object, you can quickly select a group to place your object in by clicking the right one in the available dropdown. The group has to be created first.'
3. In any group, you can select one or multiple objects and select 'Actions' to create a new group, copy or move the objects to another group, or delete from the current one.
